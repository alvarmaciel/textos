Popcorn Activity

From Technophobic to Tech-Savvy with Popcorn

The goal of this activity is for participants to use the Popcorn Maker tool to explore and challenge some of the stereotypes about women and technology. They will create a simple popcorn video illustrating what girls can gain through using and creating with technology.

Made by the MozFest 2013 Girls in Technology Scrum Group

Steps for the Activity

  1. Send participants to the Popcorn maker to get started. Participants' videos can begin by exploring some of the negative messages women receive about the current tech industry, and express some of the fears they may have about working with technology. They can then showcase some success stories of brilliant work by women technologists who can be role models for girls and women. Lastly, they can end their videos by sharing some solutions to their fears and visions for what their futures as webmakers could look like. Here's an example.

  2. Show participants what they can do with the "Events" tab. A variety of simple components can be added to your video by simply dragging and dropping them on the screen or copying and pasting links to the original content. They can add some cool images, text annotations, animated GIFs, Wikipedia pages and even Google maps to their videos.

  3. Once the video looks good, add some mood music. Music as well as videos can be easily embedded to participants' video from the "Media" tab.

  4. When participants' videos are complete, have them choose suitable names for their projects and click "Save". They can now choose thumbnails, add relevant tags and publish their videos.

  5. Have participants show their Popcorn videos to one another and discuss what they learned. Ask them to make a note of the link to their finished video - they may be using it later!

Materials

  • Computer
  • Web Access
  • Enthusiasm
  • Creativity
  • Popcorn Cheat Sheet

Discussion

  • Participants can time each event by re-sizing the event at the "layer" area or editing the "start"/"finish" and "in"/"out" times in the box on the right while adding them to the video.
  • They can also easily crop added images by clicking on the images and then re-sizing them.
  • Once the videos are complete, discuss as a group some of the common fears, inspirations and solutions.
