Activity

Girls in Tech: Role Models

You will highlight a woman in tech that you admire: find a photo, strike a pose and share some interesting things about her life. As you do this activity, you'll be helping to create an awesome knowledge-base of women in tech that will inspire the next generation of girls.

Made by TechKim

Steps for the Activity

  1. This activity can be done individually or in a group setting - much more fun! Start by considering the Discussion Questions.

  2. Maybe you already have a woman in tech in mind or maybe you need to do a little research. Check out the Additional Resources to start your research. Once you've identified your role model:

    • Find a photo of her online & copy the URL.
    • Strike a pose and take a picture of yourself. See Jaime C. Moore's Not Just a Girl photography series for inspiration.
    • Find 3 interesting tidbits about your role model and 3 links to more information or digital media.

  3. You will be remixing the Not Just a Girl Thimble project updating it with the pics and info you collected in step 2.

    • You will need to find somewhere to upload the picture you took of yourself. Here are instructions for How to Embed an Image from Google Drive.
    • You will find instructions for where your pics and info goes inside the comment tags.
    • If this is your first time remxing a Thimble project, be sure to check out the HTML Cheatsheet and CSS Cheatsheet.
  4. Share your remix! Once you have published your Not Just a Girl remix, let us know. You can tweet about it, tagging it with #teachtheweb and #girlsintech. Also, please add the URL of your project to the Women in Tech Mopad.



TechKim inspired by the MozFest 2013 Girls in Technology Scrum Group.

Materials

  • Camera/WebCam
  • Computer
  • Web Access
  • Props/Costumes

Discussion

  • Close you eyes and imagine a computer scientist - what kind of person is in the picture that pops into your head?
  • What makes a good role model? Why do female role models matter?
  • What's your favorite thing to do with technology? Can you find a woman that helped make that tech happen?
